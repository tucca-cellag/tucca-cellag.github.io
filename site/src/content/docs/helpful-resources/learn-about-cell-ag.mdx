**Cellular agriculture** is the production of agricultural products from cultured
cells rather than from whole animals — using cell culture, tissue engineering, and
related biotechnologies. It spans two kinds of products: **cellular** products that
are *made of* cells (e.g. cultivated meat and seafood) and **acellular** products
that are *made by* cells but don't contain them (e.g. dairy and egg proteins from
precision fermentation).

For the authoritative definition and TUCCA's mission, see the
[Tufts University Center for Cellular Agriculture's About page](https://cellularagriculture.tufts.edu/about).

## Why it matters

- **Sustainability** — potential for lower greenhouse-gas emissions and reduced land
  and water use versus conventional animal agriculture.
- **Ethics** — alternatives that reduce reliance on animal slaughter.
- **Food security** — production less dependent on arable land and climate.
- **Innovation** — drives advances in bioprocess engineering, cell biology, and the
  AI/computational methods at the heart of this hub.
